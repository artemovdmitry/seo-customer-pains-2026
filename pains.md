# The eight pains, ranked

Each pain carries: frequency band, intensity band, fit with a portable-handover product, what brands actually say (real quotes from public sources), and supporting frequency data where it exists.

**Frequency:** High = appears in roughly every third agency-churn discussion · Medium = every fifth · Low = less often.
**Intensity:** High = "never again" · Medium = sustained frustration · Low = inconvenience.
**Handover fit:** Direct = Handoff cures it · Partial = Handoff dampens it · Indirect = Handoff doesn't address it directly.

---

## #1 — Failure to deliver promised results

- **Frequency:** High (~29% of terminations)
- **Intensity:** High
- **Handover fit:** Partial — Handoff doesn't make promises true, but it makes them auditable.

**What brands say.** Agencies promise results in months 1–3; real movement (if any) lands in months 9–12. Brands churn before the curve turns up.

> "Most companies tell clients they'll get them on the top spot of Google. They lie."
> — Reddit user, surfaced via aggregator

> "Hired three SEO experts over 8 months. Each promised the moon. None delivered. Finally found one provider that actually moved metrics — but it cost 5× the budget I had originally allocated."
> — Trustpilot review, SEO services category

> "Inconsistent communication, missed deadlines, and lack of accountability."
> — Trustpilot review of PageTraffic

**Frequency data.** Failure to hit long-term targets accounts for ~29% of all agency terminations (Marketers Center, Seo.co).

**Why Handoff helps only partially.** Handoff can't make promises true. It can make them legible: every commitment lives as a task with an expected outcome and a measured actual outcome, so "you said +15 positions; we observed –2" becomes a contract issue rather than a he-said-she-said.

## #2 — Lack of transparency / templated reporting

- **Frequency:** High (~25% of terminations)
- **Intensity:** High
- **Handover fit:** Strong — the platform IS the source of truth.

**What brands say.** Templated PDF reports with the same layout every month, only the dates change. Vague jargon instead of work performed. When traffic drops 20%, the report leads with positive metrics.

> "Templated PDF reports that look the same every month with different dates plugged in."
> — Centori.io industry analysis

> "When traffic drops 20% but the agency leads the report with positive metrics, clients notice when they avoid discussing obvious problems."
> — Search Engine Land / Analytics That Profit

> "If you're paying for SEO, you must own the data and should have direct logins (not just screenshots)."
> — EWR Digital, summarising industry standard

**Frequency data.** Secrecy and lack of transparency = ~25% of all terminations and the leading cause of termination within the first six months. Smaller businesses are the most likely to fire over this (Marketers Center).

**Why Handoff cures it.** In Handoff, the project is owned by the brand and the agency is invited, not the other way around. There is no "agency report" — there's a live workspace with the brand-owned data, and the agency's work shows up as tasks with measurable outcomes.

## #3 — Account / data ownership confusion

- **Frequency:** High
- **Intensity:** High
- **Handover fit:** Direct — this is the core feature.

**What brands say.** The agency holds Google Analytics, Search Console, Ahrefs/Semrush, and ad accounts under the agency's own login. When the relationship ends, the brand loses access to historical data. There are no direct logins, only screenshots. The lost data on agency switch makes optimisation impossible.

> "Clients should exercise caution if their agency holds full ownership of assets, data and logins."
> — Industry contract guide

> "If you want to bring marketing in-house or switch agencies, the transition becomes complicated when you don't own the accounts, which can result in lost historical data and optimization of work."
> — solvlegal.com, on contract law applied to SEO

**Connecting fact.** Account-ownership is the single most-discussed clause in every SEO contract guide. Each guide devotes a section to who owns the accounts, content, and backlinks at termination.

**Why Handoff cures it.** Asset escrow is a first-class concept. Every credential, account, and link has a status (`TRANSFERRED` / `ESCROW` / `PENDING`) and a transition workflow. The default is brand ownership; agency access is granted, scoped, and revocable.

## #4 — Lost rankings/traffic during agency transition

- **Frequency:** Medium
- **Intensity:** Very high (documented losses in eight figures)
- **Handover fit:** Direct — this is the core thesis.

**What brands say.** At an agency switch, the old strategy is abandoned. The new strategy is applied without a bridge. Technical changes ship without redirects. Content rewrites high-performing pages without proper preservation. The result is a 20–60% traffic drop with a six-to-twelve-month recovery curve. One documented case: $12M in lost sales after a 60% traffic drop.

> "A new company suggested a redesign, totally changed the content and all the page elements without any redirection, and the client lost all their rankings and traffic."
> — Documented case via Ranked.ai

> "$12M in sales lost, traffic diminished to 40% of original size. With conservative projections — no recourse to recover."
> — Industry case study

> "If the old plan focused on local and the new one targets broader national searches, there's usually a gap. Pages that used to hold steady might get replaced with ones that take longer to rank."
> — Ranked.ai analysis

**Frequency check.** Roughly 80% of agency new-business comes from clients who left a previous agency. Constant churn implies a high frequency of transitions; the per-transition pain is dramatic but not universal.

**Why Handoff cures it.** Rank-safety SLA + 14-day escrow window during a switch. The incoming agency can't ship a redesign or rewrite high-traffic pages until the rank-safety SLA closes; the outgoing agency keeps read-only access to remediate emergencies. The bundle ensures the new team inherits the *why* behind every decision, not just the artifacts.

## #5 — Backlink / content ownership disputes

- **Frequency:** Medium
- **Intensity:** High
- **Handover fit:** Direct — link inventory and content briefs are core bundle objects.

**What brands say.** The agency claims ownership of content and backlinks, which becomes a red flag for the brand. Standard practice is that the brand owns the work after full payment, but this requires contract clarity. Disputes are common when relationships end.

> "If the agency claims ownership of content, backlinks, or other assets, this can be a red flag for clients to reconsider signing."
> — Stan Ventures contract guide

> "Same day after he fired his SEO firm, his site went missing from Google — completely missing. The SEO firm could have blocked spiders and issued a removal request via Google Webmaster Tools."
> — Search Engine Roundtable, agency-failure horror story

**Why Handoff cures it.** Every backlink in the bundle has an `acquisition` block (method, cost, vendor, campaign) and an escrow status. Every brief has a writer vendor ID and a delivery timestamp. Content drafts are referenced by URL, not held captive. Disputes become factual rather than rhetorical.

## #6 — Unethical SEO practices (black-hat tactics)

- **Frequency:** Medium
- **Intensity:** Very high (Google manual penalties)
- **Handover fit:** Indirect — Handoff's audit module can detect, not prevent.

**What brands say.** Agencies use private blog networks (PBN), paid links, content spinning, automated link-building. Quick wins land in months 1–6; manual penalties hit in months 7–12; recovery takes the rest of the year.

> "Unethical practice is most likely to get you dropped during the 7–12 month period, suggesting less scrupulous agencies are taking advantage of the 'honeymoon period' of their new contract to earn 'quick wins'."
> — Marketers Center research

**Frequency data.** Roughly 10% of all terminations correlate with Google manual penalties.

**Why Handoff helps only indirectly.** Handoff's audit module surfaces link-profile anomalies (sudden DR spikes, anchor over-optimisation, suspicious referring-domain patterns), but it cannot stop an agency from running a PBN. What it can do is preserve the disavow file and rationale across transitions so the next agency doesn't blunder back into the same trap.

## #7 — Strategy not aligned with the brand's GTM

- **Frequency:** Medium
- **Intensity:** Medium
- **Handover fit:** Partial — modular roadmap helps, but doesn't replace strategic thinking.

**What brands say.** Agencies apply cookie-cutter playbooks regardless of brand specifics. SEO isn't integrated with paid, content, or product strategy. CMOs see effort that isn't aligned with the business plan.

> "SEO that is not tightly aligned with your go-to-market motion is just wasted spend."
> — Search Engine Journal, CMO trends piece

> "Inconsistent strategy, outdated tactics, and lack of technical expertise can hold marketers back from owning their category in search."
> — CMSWire, State of CMO 2025

**Why Handoff helps partially.** The modular roadmap lets a brand assemble an SEO plan from typed task-blocks (SERP, ASO, Local, AI-search) keyed to its GTM motion. It doesn't write the strategy, but it gives the strategy somewhere structured to live, so misalignment becomes visible early rather than at month nine.

## #8 — AI-search disruption / vendor can't adapt

- **Frequency:** Low (growing fast)
- **Intensity:** Existential
- **Handover fit:** Direct — Handoff has AI-search GEO/AEO as a first-class roadmap module.

**What brands say.** Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT/Perplexity are eating the top of the click funnel. Traditional SEO agencies don't understand GEO/AEO. CMOs are under pressure to adapt; agencies are locked into the old playbook.

> "Enterprise CMOs and marketing directors grappling with technical SEO issues and wanting to pioneer AI-driven optimization are increasingly looking for agencies equipped to partner closely with in-house teams."
> — Xponent21 industry analysis

**Why it matters now.** Roughly 15% of Google searches now show AI Overviews. Brand visibility in LLM answers is a new measurement problem. Agencies without GEO/AEO tooling look obsolete.

**Why Handoff cures it.** AI-search GEO/AEO is a typed module in the modular roadmap. Citations across ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Perplexity are tracked alongside Google rankings. When a brand finds itself with an agency that can't adapt, switching to one that can is a 14-day operation, not a six-month rebuild.

## Honourable mentions (didn't make the top 8)

- **Slow communication / SLA missed.** Real, but usually a symptom of #1 or #2.
- **Pricing opacity.** Real, but typically resolved by switching, not by a tool.
- **Onboarding friction.** Annoying, not a churn driver in the data.
- **Off-platform reporting (Looker Studio sprawl).** A pain for agency PMs more than for brands.

These show up in the data but don't reach the threshold for inclusion.
