# SEO Customer Pains (2026)

> Why brands fire SEO agencies, ranked. Notes from desk research before building [Handoff](https://handoff-smooth-transitions.lovable.app/).

This repo documents the eight most-cited pains brands have with SEO agencies, ranked by frequency × intensity, with sources cited inline. It exists because before building a "fire-your-SEO-agency-without-losing-rankings" product I needed to be honest about whether the pain I'd been hearing in 16 years of SEO work was a real, ranked, addressable wedge — or just my professional bubble.

The short version: agency churn is the symptom; account/asset/decision lock-in is the root cause. The rank-loss-on-transition pain is rare *per brand* but catastrophic when it hits — documented case losses run into eight figures.

## Top-line numbers

| Cause of agency termination | Share of all terminations |
|---|---|
| Failure to deliver promised results | ~29% |
| Lack of transparency / templated reporting | ~25% |
| Account/data ownership confusion | high (top contract-clause concern) |
| Lost rankings/traffic during transition | medium frequency, very high intensity ($12M+ losses on record) |
| Backlink/content ownership disputes | medium frequency, high intensity |
| Unethical SEO (PBN, paid links) | ~10% (manual penalties) |
| Strategy not aligned with GTM | medium frequency, medium intensity |
| AI-search disruption / vendor can't adapt | low frequency now, growing fast, existential intensity |

Sources: Marketers Center, Seo.co, Search Engine Roundtable, Trustpilot reviews of named SEO agencies, contract guides at solvlegal.com and Stan Ventures, industry analyses at Centori.io, Search Engine Land / Analytics That Profit, Ranked.ai, EWR Digital, SEJ, CMSWire, Xponent21.

Every pain page in [`pains.md`](./pains.md) carries its own quotes and citations.

## What I'm doing with this

Building [Handoff](https://handoff-smooth-transitions.lovable.app/) — a portable handover protocol so brands can switch SEO agencies without losing rankings. The pains in this repo map to specific Handoff features:

| Pain | Handoff response |
|---|---|
| #2 Transparency | Project ownership = client by default; agency invited; data is the single source of truth |
| #3 Account ownership | Asset escrow with TRANSFERRED / ESCROW / PENDING status on every credential, link, and brief |
| #4 Lost rankings on transition | Rank-safe SLA + 14-day escrow window during agency switch |
| #5 Backlink/content ownership | Link inventory is part of the bundle, signed by both parties |
| #8 AI-search disruption | Modular roadmap with AI-search GEO/AEO blocks |

Pains #1, #6, and #7 are partial-fits — Handoff doesn't directly cure "the agency promised the moon", but it makes promises auditable and decisions reversible.
