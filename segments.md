# Pains by customer segment

Same eight pains, but the priority order changes a lot by segment. This matters for go-to-market: the wedge for E-commerce DTC is "rank-loss on transition" (#4); the wedge for B2B SaaS is "AI-search disruption" (#8); the wedge for SMB is "transparency" (#2).

## E-commerce DTC ($2–50M GMV)

| Rank | Pain |
|---|---|
| 1 | Lost rankings/traffic on transition (#4) |
| 2 | Failure to deliver promised results (#1) |
| 3 | Lack of transparency / templated reporting (#2) |

**Why this segment is the wedge.** Every percentage point of organic traffic is revenue. A 30% drop during a botched transition is a quantifiable, reportable event with a dollar figure attached. This is the most pain-aware audience for a "rank-safe" pitch.

**Buying motion.** Founder or head-of-marketing decides; agency-of-record relationship. Sales cycle 4–8 weeks. ARPA $24k–$60k.

## B2B SaaS (post-Series A)

| Rank | Pain |
|---|---|
| 1 | Strategy not aligned with product GTM (#7) |
| 2 | AI-search disruption / vendor can't adapt (#8) |
| 3 | Account ownership / lock-in (#3) |

**Why this segment buys early.** SaaS founders are technical, read Reddit and Hacker News, and recognise the lock-in pattern from infrastructure (it's `package.json` for SEO). They also feel AI-search pressure first because their target buyers are early adopters of LLM-based research.

**Buying motion.** VP Marketing decides with founder approval. Sales cycle 6–10 weeks. ARPA $36k–$120k.

## SMB local services ($0.5–5M revenue)

| Rank | Pain |
|---|---|
| 1 | Failure to deliver promised results (#1) |
| 2 | Lack of transparency / templated reporting (#2) |
| 3 | Pricing opacity (honourable mention) |

**Why we're not starting here.** Low willingness to pay. The "SEO is a scam" perception is concentrated here — and a portable-handover product reads as "for big enough brands to bother handing over". Volume is high but per-account economics are bad.

**Future fit.** Once the platform has a self-serve free tier (the "SEO Reality Check" wedge), this segment becomes addressable.

## Mid-market enterprise ($50–500M revenue)

| Rank | Pain |
|---|---|
| 1 | AI-search adaptation pressure (#8) |
| 2 | Strategy alignment with multi-channel marketing (#7) |
| 3 | Reporting transparency for board meetings (#2) |

**Why this segment buys late.** Procurement, security review, vendor consolidation pressure. But ACV justifies the cycle: $120k–$400k contracts.

**Buying motion.** CMO with procurement gating. Sales cycle 12–24 weeks. Land typically as a "switch-agency support" engagement; expand into modular roadmap and AI-search modules.

## Cross-segment patterns

Three patterns hold across all segments:

1. **Account ownership confusion (#3) is universal.** It just shows up earlier in the buying conversation for B2B SaaS and later for SMB.
2. **Transition pain (#4) is rare per-brand but catastrophic when it hits.** Every segment has at least one war story that drives the conversation forward.
3. **AI-search (#8) is climbing fast.** In Q4 2024 it was an honourable mention; by Q1 2026 it is a top-three pain for every segment except SMB local.

These three are the durable wedges. The other five pains are real but better treated as expansion conversations than as opening hooks.
